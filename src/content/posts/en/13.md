We are immersed, surrounded, and fused with technology in a landscape characterized by rapid evolution and increasingly distributed teams, where technical skills now represent only part of professional success. Knowing how to design solid software architectures or develop them is crucial, but without the ability to relate to others, the value of these skills risks being wasted. This is where soft skills come in, those interpersonal and behavioral abilities that allow effective collaboration, facing challenges, and growing within a team.

##### Why are they fundamental?

A successful technical team is not just a group of people with high technical skills, but a network of professionals who collaborate, share ideas, and solve problems together, and soft skills make this interaction possible. One of the most sought-after abilities is the capacity to explain complex ideas clearly and understandably, both to technical colleagues and non-technical stakeholders, because communicating effectively not only reduces misunderstandings, but also accelerates problem resolution, creating a more productive and collaborative work environment.

Teamwork is a constant in the technology world, and collaborating effectively means building mutual trust, showing respect towards others, and knowing how to accept and provide constructive feedback. This dynamic not only improves the quality of work, but also fosters an environment where everyone can give their best: a calm setting, where you can be professional even while sharing a joke, is far more productive than one where the fear of saying something wrong or confronting a more experienced colleague prevails.

Knowing how to organize one's work, set the right priorities, and meet deadlines is fundamental to ensuring project success without accumulating excessive stress. Equally important is the ability to adapt, because technologies change rapidly and so do project requirements. Creative problem solving, often considered a technical skill, is in fact among the most valuable soft skills, because creativity in finding innovative solutions can make the difference in complex situations.

##### What happens in a technological team?

Imagine a software development project where a multidisciplinary team works together to create a mobile application: each person brings specific skills, but without effective communication the project risks derailing. Developers might not fully understand the product requirements, designers might not be aligned with the technical functionalities, and project managers might struggle to keep everyone on the same page. Often what is missing is the sharing of long-term objectives, critical deadlines, or emerging difficulties, and in those moments it is better to reach the goal quickly and optimize later rather than fall behind schedule while chasing technological perfection.

In these contexts, soft skills become the glue that holds everything together. A team leader with strong communication skills can translate technical language into terms everyone understands, facilitating collaboration, while a team member who listens actively can identify potential problems before they become obstacles.

##### How to improve them?

Soft skills are not innate, but they can be developed with effort and practice. Learning active listening, paying attention without interrupting, helps better understand others' needs and points of view, and requesting feedback on one's behaviors is an excellent way to identify areas for improvement. Numerous courses and workshops exist dedicated to developing these skills, from communication to time management. Practicing emotional awareness, recognizing and managing one's emotions, is fundamental to maintaining calm in stressful situations, and getting involved in group projects, even outside of work, can help strengthen teamwork skills.

##### Hard skills and soft skills: a matter of balance

While hard skills are often the reason we get hired, soft skills are the reason we stay. A technical professional who masters both dimensions becomes an irreplaceable asset: an excellent programmer who is unable to collaborate can slow down a project or deteriorate the team's climate from within, while a programmer with average skills but strong interpersonal abilities contributes to creating a more productive and harmonious work environment. Soft skills are an essential component of success in the technology world because they not only improve interpersonal relationships, but also allow for better handling of daily challenges and standing out in a competitive sector.
